# Business Model

AWO Platform operates a diversified revenue model designed to build sustainable profitability while serving the underbanked women's market across SADC. Our approach combines transaction-based fees, asset management revenues, and value-added services to create multiple income streams that scale with user growth and engagement.

## Executive Summary

<Info>
**Business Model Overview**: AWO operates a freemium platform with multiple revenue streams including investment management fees, loan origination commissions, wealth coaching services, and premium feature subscriptions. The model prioritizes long-term customer value over short-term extraction, building trust and loyalty in underserved communities.
</Info>

**Key Financial Targets (5-Year Horizon):**
- **Revenue Growth**: R10M (Year 1) → R500M (Year 5)
- **User Base**: 50K (Year 1) → 2M (Year 5)
- **Assets Under Management**: R100M → R10B
- **Gross Margin**: 65%+ across all revenue streams
- **Customer Acquisition Cost**: &lt;R50 with R500+ LTV

### Financial Risk Controls

**Revenue Quality Assurance:**
- Maximum 20% of revenue from any single customer or partner
- Diversified payment provider relationships to reduce processing risk
- Quarterly revenue recognition audits by independent accounting firm
- Real-time financial monitoring and alert systems

**Credit and Investment Risk Management:**
- Maximum 2% platform funds at risk in any single SME investment
- Diversified investment product portfolio through multiple asset managers
- Regular stress testing of investment performance scenarios
- Professional indemnity insurance for investment advisory services

## Strategic Partnerships and Revenue Sharing

### Partnership Revenue Models

**Banking and Payment Partners:**
- **Revenue Share**: 15-25% of transaction fees with banking partners
- **Integration Bonuses**: Performance-based bonuses for API uptime and user satisfaction
- **Volume Discounts**: Declining fee structures as transaction volumes increase

**Investment Product Partners:**
- **Management Fee Split**: 60/40 split with AWO taking 40% of management fees
- **Performance Fee Sharing**: 50/50 split on performance fees above benchmark returns
- **Minimum Guarantees**: Annual minimum revenue guarantees from major asset managers

**SME Platform Partners:**
- **Origination Fee Sharing**: 70/30 split with AWO taking 70% of origination fees
- **Due Diligence Cost Recovery**: Partners cover 100% of due diligence costs
- **Risk Sharing**: 90/10 first-loss protection with partners taking senior risk

## Conclusion

AWO Platform's business model is designed for sustainable, profitable growth while serving a traditionally underserved market segment. The diversified revenue approach reduces risk while the focus on community-driven growth creates powerful network effects and customer loyalty.

**Key Business Model Strengths:**

1. **Multiple Revenue Streams**: Reduces dependence on any single income source
2. **High-Margin Recurring Revenue**: Investment management and subscriptions provide predictable income
3. **Community Network Effects**: Each new user and chama increases value for existing users
4. **Scalable Technology Platform**: Low marginal costs for serving additional users
5. **Mission-Market Alignment**: Strong customer loyalty through meaningful financial impact

**Financial Trajectory:**
The model projects path to R900M+ annual revenue by Year 5 with healthy 25%+ EBITDA margins, driven by platform scale effects and increasing user sophistication. The combination of freemium accessibility and premium value-added services ensures broad market penetration while capturing maximum value from high-engagement users.

This approach positions AWO for potential exit opportunities through strategic acquisition by major financial institutions seeking African market exposure or IPO consideration as a leading financial technology platform serving women's economic empowerment.
