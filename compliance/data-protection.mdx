## South Africa POPIA Compliance

### POPIA Foundation and Scope

The Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) is South Africa's comprehensive data protection law, fully enforced since July 1, 2021. POPIA protects the personal information of both natural persons and juristic persons (where applicable).

**POPIA Application to AWO Platform**:
- **Territorial Scope**: All processing of personal information within South Africa
- **Extraterritorial Application**: Processing by entities worldwide using automated or non-automated means in South Africa
- **Broad Definition**: Personal information includes any information relating to an identifiable person or entity
- **Financial Services Focus**: Enhanced obligations for financial institutions handling sensitive financial data

## Privacy Impact Assessments

### DPIA Framework and Process

**When DPIAs are Required**:
AWO Platform conducts Data Protection Impact Assessments for:
- High-risk processing activities involving sensitive personal data
- Large-scale systematic monitoring of individuals
- Automated decision-making with significant effects
- New technologies or innovative processing methods
- Cross-border data transfers to countries without adequate protection

**DPIA Methodology**:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Preparation and Scoping">
    **Project Definition**: Clear definition of processing activities and purposes  
    **Stakeholder Engagement**: Involvement of relevant internal and external stakeholders  
    **Legal Review**: Assessment of legal basis and compliance requirements  
    **Risk Context**: Understanding of broader risk context and business objectives
  </Step>
  <Step title="Risk Assessment">
    **Data Flow Mapping**: Comprehensive mapping of personal data flows  
    **Threat Identification**: Identification of potential privacy threats and vulnerabilities  
    **Impact Analysis**: Assessment of potential impact on data subjects  
    **Risk Rating**: Systematic risk rating using established risk methodology
  </Step>
  <Step title="Risk Mitigation">
    **Control Design**: Design of appropriate technical and organizational controls  
    **Risk Treatment**: Implementation of risk treatment measures  
    **Residual Risk Assessment**: Assessment of residual risk after controls  
    **Acceptance Criteria**: Clear criteria for risk acceptance or further mitigation
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review and Monitoring">
    **Regular Review**: Periodic review and update of DPIA findings  
    **Control Effectiveness**: Monitoring effectiveness of implemented controls  
    **Change Management**: Update DPIA for significant changes to processing  
    **Continuous Improvement**: Integration of lessons learned into DPIA process
  </Step>
</Steps>
